Ernest Gary Gygax

"Father of role-playing games"

Alan De Smet [CC BY 3.0 or CC BY 2.5], via Wikimedia Commons

Timeline

  • 1938 Born in Chicago, Illinois, United States

  • 1960's Founded the Gen Con gaming convention, and created an organization of wargaming clubs.

  • 1971 Assisted in the development of Chainmail, a minuratures wargame, based upon medieval warfare.

  • 1973 Co-founded Tactical Studies Rules(TSR, Inc.), with childhood friend, Don Kaye.

  • 1974 Created Dungeons & Dragons, with Dave Arneson, which expanded Chainmail, and included fantasy story elements.

  • 1974 Founded The Dragon, a magazine about the new game.

  • 1977 Began work on Advanced Dungeons & Dragons, a more comprehensive version of the game system, with many manuals and pre-packaged adventures.
